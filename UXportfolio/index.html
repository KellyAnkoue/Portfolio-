About

Design isn’t just how something looks

It’s who it includes and who it leaves out. That’s the lens I bring to every project.

I have a B.A. in Information Science from Cornell, with concentrations in UX Design and Law, Ethics & Policy, and an M.A. in Ethics & Society from Fordham. Before design, I advocated on climate and equity policy, led programs for hundreds of students, and taught UX methodology at Cornell.

I’m drawn to problems where the stakes are high and the users are the most overlooked. Healthcare policy, institutional barriers, creator trust - these aren’t abstract design briefs. They’re the things I’ve spent the last four years researching, advocating for, and now designing around.
